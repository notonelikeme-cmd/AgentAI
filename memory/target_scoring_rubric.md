# Target Scoring Rubric

## Scoring System (0-100)

Score each potential target company based on the following criteria. Higher scores = better outreach candidates.

### 1. Security Posture Weakness (0-30 points)
- 25-30: Multiple obvious security gaps, weak headers, exposed endpoints, no HTTPS enforcement
- 18-24: Clear weaknesses in authentication, API security, or session handling
- 12-17: Some security issues visible, outdated dependencies or patterns
- 6-11: Minor issues only, generally decent posture but room for improvement
- 0-5: Strong security posture, fewer actionable findings

### 2. Business Impact / Risk Exposure (0-20 points)
- 18-20: High-profile, handles sensitive user data, compliance-critical industry (finance, healthcare, SaaS)
- 14-17: Medium-high exposure, handles customer data, moderate brand/financial impact
- 10-13: Some exposure, consumer-facing or data-handling, moderate impact if breached
- 6-9: Lower exposure, internal tools or non-critical systems
- 0-5: Very low exposure, minimal business impact

### 3. Company Size & Budget (0-15 points)
- 13-15: Enterprise (1000+ employees) or significant revenue ($100M+), strong budget for security
- 10-12: Mid-market (100-1000 employees) or $10M-$100M revenue, reasonable security budget
- 7-9: Small-to-mid (20-100 employees) or $1M-$10M revenue, limited but available budget
- 4-6: Startup (5-20 employees) or <$1M revenue, minimal budget but potential
- 0-3: Pre-revenue or very small, unlikely to pay

### 4. Visibility & Reputation (0-15 points)
- 13-15: Known brand, public presence, reputation damage from breach would be significant
- 10-12: Recognizable in their industry, moderate reputation risk
- 7-9: Decent online presence, some reputation risk
- 4-6: Low visibility, minimal reputation impact
- 0-3: Unknown or very low profile

### 5. Bug Bounty / Disclosure History (0-10 points)
- 8-10: Not on major bug bounty platforms, no known vulnerability disclosure program (better outreach candidate)
- 6-7: Minimal bug bounty presence, underserved
- 4-5: Some bug bounty presence but not active
- 2-3: Active bug bounty program (more competition)
- 0-1: Large, mature bug bounty program (heavy competition)

### 6. Industry / Sector (0-10 points)
- 9-10: Finance, healthcare, SaaS, fintech, cybersecurity (high-risk, high-compliance)
- 7-8: E-commerce, retail, logistics, real estate (moderate risk)
- 5-6: Media, education, B2B services
- 3-4: Government, non-profit, low-risk sectors
- 0-2: Entertainment, entertainment-only, minimal security need

## Score Interpretation

- 85-100: Excellent outreach candidate, likely to convert, strong business case
- 70-84: Good candidate, worth outreach, reasonable conversion likelihood
- 55-69: Moderate candidate, worth exploring, less certain conversion
- 40-54: Lower priority, outreach may be worth it but less likely to convert
- <40: Not recommended for outreach at this time

## Outreach Confidence

Combine score with:
- Decision-maker accessibility (easy to find security contact?)
- Company's known growth/security investment trajectory
- Likelihood of authorization for paid review vs. bug bounty

Score 70+ = prioritize for outreach
Score 55-69 = secondary tier, outreach if capacity permits
Score <55 = only if unique opportunity or specific vulnerability knowledge
